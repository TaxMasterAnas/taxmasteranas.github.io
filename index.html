  • ITR-1 (Sahaj):
    For individuals with income up to ₹50 lakh from salary, one house property, and other sources (interest, etc.), but not for those with business income.
  • ITR-2:
    For individuals and HUFs not having income from business or profession, but having income from salary, more than one house property, capital gains, and other sources.
  • ITR-3:
    For individuals and HUFs having income from a proprietary business or profession, along with income from other sources.
  • ITR-4 (Sugam):
    For individuals, HUFs, and firms (other than LLP) with a total income up to ₹50 lakh and having income from business and profession, computed under the presumptive taxation scheme.
  • ITR-5:
    For partnership firms, LLPs, Association of Persons (AOPs), Body of Individuals (BOIs), and other similar entities.
  • ITR-6:
    For companies other than those claiming exemption under section 11 (income from property held for charitable or religious purposes).
  • ITR-7:
    For persons including companies that are required to file returns under sections 139(4A), 139(4B), 139(4C), or 139(4D), typically applicable to trusts, political parties, and scientific research institutions.

  • Accounting (Managing & Recording):
    Involves the systematic recording, summarizing, and reporting of financial transactions to track a company’s financial status.
  • Ensure Payment & Receipts:
    Managing and verifying all incoming and outgoing payments to ensure accurate financial records and cash flow.
  • Updating Ledger:
    Regularly recording transactions into the ledger, ensuring that financial statements reflect accurate and up-to-date information.
  • Banking:
    Handling all bank-related transactions, including deposits, withdrawals, and bank reconciliations, to maintain accurate cash balances.
  • Costing:
    Analyzing costs associated with production or operations to help in pricing, budgeting, and financial planning.
  • Profit and Loss:
    Preparing the profit and loss statement, which summarizes revenues, costs, and expenses to determine net profit or loss.
  • Balance Sheet:
    A financial statement that provides a snapshot of a company’s assets, liabilities, and equity at a specific point in time.
  • Audit:
    The systematic review and verification of financial records to ensure accuracy, compliance, and transparency in financial reporting.

  • IEC Code:
    A unique 10-digit code issued by the Directorate General of Foreign Trade (DGFT) required for businesses to import or export goods in India.
  • Import Accounting:
    The process of recording and managing financial transactions related to the purchase of goods or services from foreign countries, including duties, taxes, and shipping costs.
  • Export Accounting:
    The process of recording and managing financial transactions related to the sale of goods or services to foreign countries, including revenue recognition and compliance with export regulations.
  • GST Refund:
    The process of claiming a refund on the Goods and Services Tax (GST) paid on inputs used in the production of goods or services that are exported, to avoid double taxation.

  • GST Registration:
    The process of registering a business under the Goods and Services Tax system to obtain a unique GSTIN, which is necessary for collecting and remitting GST.
  • GST Return Filing:
    The process of submitting periodic statements containing details of sales, purchases, tax collected, and tax paid to the tax authorities under the GST system.
  • GST R1:
    A monthly or quarterly return filed to report the outward supplies (sales) made by a business, including details of invoices and the taxes collected.
  • GST R3B:
    A summary return filed monthly to declare the summary of sales, purchases, input tax credit, and tax liability for a given period.
  • GST R9:
    An annual return that consolidates all the information from the monthly/quarterly returns (GSTR-1, GSTR-3B) filed during the financial year, providing a complete overview of the business's GST activities.

  • TDS Payment:
    The process of deducting tax at the source of income, such as salaries, interest, or contract payments, and depositing it with the government on behalf of the payee.
  • TDS/TCS Filing:
    The submission of quarterly returns detailing the tax deducted (TDS) or collected (TCS) and deposited with the government, including information on the deductor, deductee, and payment details.
  • TDS Compliance:
    Ensuring adherence to all legal requirements and deadlines related to TDS, including accurate deduction, timely payment, and filing of returns to avoid penalties.
